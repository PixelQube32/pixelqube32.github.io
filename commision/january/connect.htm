Annual Meeting Celebrates Our Accomplishments

Every November, the Collaboration Council's partners and friends cometogether to celebrate our collective accomplishments over the past fiscal year. This year, 200 guests were greetedand served appetizers and desserts by our youth volunteers from Healthy Living Lifestyle and Center for Adoption Support and Education. To shocase the Collaboration Council's work, service providers joined together to present the Report to the Community. Featured were featured Healthy Families Montgomery, Arts on the Block, Gandhi Brigade, One Dream Families for Children's Mental Health.

Throughout the program, our board members presented the Collaboration Council's Award for Time Well Spent On Behalf of Montgomery's Children to Teresa Bennett, Art Billings, Arva Jackson, Senator Nancy King, Brenda Petersen, and The Gazette. Aspecial thank you to our top sponsors for their continued support of our work that strengthens the community, and enshures that children and families get the services and supports they need to lead healthy and succesful lives: The Gazette, Adventist Health Care, M&T Bank, and Maryland Choices.     Did You Know?

    Did you know strong families help protect children from a life of crime? We need to stop risky behaviors from escalating. We need to prevent future involvement with police. We need to support families who are struggling with poverty, and isolation created by cultural/language barriers. We are doing just that by wrapping community services around children and their families, thanks to funding from the Governor's Office for Crime Control and Prevention.
